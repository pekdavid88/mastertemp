Roda is a lovely, traditional fishing village situated 38 kilometres Northwest of Corfu Town (Kerkyra), on the northern tip of the Ionian Islands. It features one of the longest sand and shingle beaches with shallow, safe waters for swimming. The beach stretches for 4 kilometres along the village and has been proudly awarded a Blue Flag.

Island Discovery

Explore Corfu

Corfu in Greece is strongly influenced by the Venetians, the French and the English and for this reason has a cosmopolitan, multicultural charm. One of the most beautiful and romantic islands of the Ionian island group, it offers everything from lush green hills to noble mansions, idyllic beaches, and fascinating museums.

Strongly influenced by its diverse history, Corfu Town is a brilliant base for exploration. Get lost in the narrow cobblestone alleys, the so-called “kandounia”, visit world-class museums like the Museum of Asian Art, walk down the famous Liston promenade, or stroll around the huge Spianada square. The entire town exudes a nostalgic, glorious past visible in imposing palaces, noble mansions, and charming historic squares.

Moving away from the historic center, you’ll discover picturesque seaside villages, tiny verdant islets, luxurious resorts, and wild, untouched natural landscapes. It’s truly amazing how the different sides of this idyllic island coexist in perfect harmony, ready to please any type of traveler.

Coastal Wonders

The Best Beaches of Corfu

With crystal-clear waters and surrounded by lush greenery, the beaches of Corfu attract travelers from all over the world. Many are fully organized with seaside restaurants, water sports, and beach bars, while others remain untouched, waiting to be discovered.

23 km from Town

Paleokastritsa Beach

Diving Club Sandy Family Friendly Organised Water sports

Paleokastritsa is the most famous and probably most picturesque beach resort of the island. Huge, green cliffs surround the coast and many sea caves can be visited by boat around the area.

16 km from Town

Agios Gordios Beach

Sandy Family Friendly Organised

The beach of Agios Gordios is one of the most popular beaches in Corfu. The wide sandy beach is surrounded by impressive rock formations, vibrant Mediterranean greenery, and excellent tourist facilities.

17 km from Town

Myrtiotissa Beach

Partly Organized Sandy Nudism Friendly

This beach owns its name to the beautiful Monastery of Virgin Mary Myrtiotissa located just above it. It is a stunning, unorganised beach, totally unspoiled by modern tourism and unofficially nudist.

20 km from Town

Rovinia Beach

Pebbled Secluded Non Organised

Rovinia is an exotic, hidden paradise located in the Paleokastritsa region. It is reachable only by boat or through a scenic hiking path from the nearby village of Liapades.

37 km from Town

Bataria Kassiopi Beach

Sandy Family Friendly Organised

A small, clean, and perfectly organized cove near Kassiopi village. Boasting emerald waters and smooth pebbles, it gets very popular in the summer with swimmers and snorkeling enthusiasts.

Cultural Heritage

Places to Visit in Corfu

Every summer, Corfu island attracts thousands of tourists and has become a favorite destination for summer holidays. The historical and religious monuments, the picturesque villages, the beautiful nature, and the hospitality of the locals offer memories difficult to forget. Explore the must-see cultural gems of the island.

Kanoni & Mouse Island

The region of Kanoni and the Mouse Island (Pontikonisi) is the most picturesque spot of Corfu Town. High up on a hill, there is a canon and from there people can see the Mouse Island and the little church of Panagia Vlaherna, linked with the mainland with a narrow pathway.

Achillion Palace

The Palace was built in 1890 exclusively for Elizabeth (Sissy), the Empress of Austria. It used to serve as the summer retreat of the Empress, who named it Achillion after her favorite Greek hero, Achilles. After her assassination, it was sold to the Kaiser of Germany.

Old Fortress

The Old Fortress of Corfu stands next to Spianada square and Liston. It is an impressive castle built on a peninsula into the sea and connected to the mainland by a narrow bridge. A lighthouse stands on its top and offers a great view of the town and the sea.

Spianada Square

Spianada is the most impressive square of Corfu Town and lies between the Old Fortress and the Old Town. This is the second largest square in Europe, after the famous Square of Saint Mark in Venice, radiating a truly cosmopolitan grandeur.

Municipal Art Gallery

The Municipal Gallery of Corfu is housed in the Palace of Saint Michael and Saint George. It houses brilliant works of local artists of the 19th century and frequently hosts important international and local art exhibitions.

Byzantine Museum

Housed in the church of Antivouniotissa, one of the oldest and richest churches in Corfu Town. Converted into a museum in 1979, it exhibits a rich collection of Byzantine and post-Byzantine icons and relics, representing over five centuries of religious art.
